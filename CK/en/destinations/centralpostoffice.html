Antique Architectural Masterpiece

Saigon Central Post Office is one of the typical architectural works of HCMC, built from 1886–1891 by Villedieu and Foulhoux. The building has a Gothic style, harmoniously combined with Asian decorative features, creating a unique beauty.

  • Address: 02 Cong xa Paris, Ben Nghe Ward, District 1.
  • Opening Hours: 07:00 - 19:00 (daily)

Architectural Highlights

Upon entering, visitors will be immediately impressed by the large arched structure in the main hall. The decorative details are carefully preserved:

  • Vaulted Ceiling: Creates a sense of vast, ancient space, supported by an intricate iron pillar system.
  • Two Historical Maps: On the walls on both sides of the entrance are two old maps: "Saigon et ses environs" (Saigon and its surroundings, 1892) and "Lignes télégraphiques du Sud Vietnam et du Cambodge" (Telegraph lines of South Vietnam and Cambodia, 1936).
  • Telephone Booths: The wooden public telephone booths are still preserved, reminiscent of a bygone era of communication.
  • Souvenir Counters: Besides postal functions, it also sells many unique souvenirs and postcards.

Visitor Tips

Note: The post office is right next to the Notre Dame Cathedral. You can combine visits to both locations. This is still an operating post office, so please keep quiet while visiting.
