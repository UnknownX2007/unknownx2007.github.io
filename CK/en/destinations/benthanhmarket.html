Saigon's Historical Symbol

Ben Thanh Market is not just a bustling shopping place but also a historical witness, an inseparable architectural symbol of Saigon - HCMC. Built in 1912, the market with its 4-sided clock tower has witnessed the city's many ups and downs.

  • Address: Le Loi Street, Ben Thanh Ward, District 1.
  • Opening Hours: 06:00 - 18:00 (Night Market area from 18:00 - 22:00)

Exploring Inside the Market

Entering the market, you will be overwhelmed by the variety of goods. The stalls are arranged scientifically by area:

  • Food Court: Where you can enjoy countless specialty dishes like bun rieu, banh beo, spring rolls, and cool sweet desserts.
  • Souvenir Area: A variety of handicrafts, ao dai, conical hats, lacquer paintings... suitable as gifts.
  • Fabric Area: A paradise of fabrics from traditional silk to modern materials.
  • Dried Goods Area: Various dried specialties, jams, and coffee.

Visitor Tips

Note: The market is often very crowded. You should take care of your personal belongings and don't be afraid to bargain (haggle) when buying souvenirs!
