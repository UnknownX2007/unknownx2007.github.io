National Historical-Cultural Relic

Independence Palace (or Reunification Palace) is a unique architectural work, associated with many major historical events of Vietnam. This is where the end of the Vietnam War was witnessed on April 30, 1975, symbolizing national reunification.

  • Address: 135 Nam Ky Khoi Nghia, Ben Thanh Ward, District 1.
  • Opening Hours: 08:00 - 16:30 (Ticket office closes at 15:30)

Exploring Inside the Palace

Visiting Independence Palace, tourists can explore the unique architecture of the building, including the main areas:

  • Fixed Area: Includes staterooms, cabinet meeting room, president's office, and entertainment rooms.
  • Thematic Area: Exhibition rooms on historical and cultural themes.
  • Palace Bunker: The location of the command center and communication system, with map rooms, communication rooms...
  • Rooftop: Displays the President's UH-1 helicopter and offers a view of the city center.

Entrance fee:

  • Adults: 40,000VND
  • Students: 20,000VND
  • Children: 10,000VND

Note:

  • Visitors should wear respectful attire when visiting.
  • You can use the audio guide service to better understand the historical value of each area.
